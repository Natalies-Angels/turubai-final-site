What is Barakoa?

Barakoa is a (23+) immersive popup experience in Eldoret. A temporary world built from temporary boxes.

It is a space to explore emotional masks. The ways we hide our true feelings from ourselves and others.

Barakoa is a ritual of release. A container to express what we often suppress in daily life: vulnerability, tenderness, fear, longing, and love.

It is designed for adults who are ready to engage emotionally, creatively, and physically — without spectacle, without pressure, and without needing to perform clarity.

Through live music, theatre, fashion, food, and ritual play, Barakoa creates space to soften what we suppress.

Core Message for Barakoa 2026:
Love, Fear & the Quiet.

Why Barakoa?

Barakoa is a Swahili word for masks. The ones we wear to survive. To belong. To stay safe. To be seen...or to disappear.

In daily life, these masks are often invisible to us. We wear them instinctively, switching them on and off depending on where we are, who we’re with, and what we fear losing.

Barakoa exists to bring awareness to these masks, and let them soften through art.

For this edition, the experience is shaped around six emotional masks that many of us move through in relationships, community, and solitude.

Barakoa is a ritual for our times. A space to feel deeply, express honestly, and connect authentically.

Why You, Specifically

Because we love your work!

Barakoa needs a sound architecture that can hold two things at once: unfamiliarity and familiarity.

We’re curating an original beat language that’s rooted, patient, and ours... while still allowing recognisable moments to surface so the room stays open and embodied.

Your work sits comfortably in that tension. It doesn’t rush resolution, but it knows how to let people in. That balance is central to how we want the Barakoa sound to feel.

The Barakoa Beat

For this edition, we’re intentionally curating an original Barakoa beat.

We're calling this beat NiloFunkTronica.

NiloFunkTronica draws from:

  • • Nilotic rhythmic intelligence and cyclical form
  • • Afro-house pacing and structure
  • • Live instrumentation, vocals, and orchestral elements
  • • Electronic textures and synthesized elements

It’s groove-led, trance-adjacent, and designed for collective movement; prioritising feel and embodiment over spectacle.

At the same time, we’re conscious of the crowd.

While the beat needs to be distinctly ours, it should still nod to familiar rhythms, hits, and bangers — not to dilute the sound, but to create moments of recognition and ease within the unfamiliar.

This balance is important to us.
