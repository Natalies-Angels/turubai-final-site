FABRi

A Turubai Fabrication Practice

where making is how we take responsibility

keep scrolling :)

FABRi is Turubai’s fabrication practice.

It works with material, form, and function to translate cultural questions into things that can be worn, touched, inhabited, or gathered around. These things live close to bodies and spaces. They carry intention. They carry context.

FABRi approaches making as a cultural act. Material choices are never neutral. They hold labour, environment, access, memory, and power. FABRi designs with these realities in mind — creating work that is functional, inclusive, and grounded in lived experience.

What Guides FABRi

Ecojustice

FABRi takes responsibility for material impact — sourcing, production, scale, and waste. The goal is not perfection, but care. Thoughtful decisions that respect environment, labour, and longevity.

Inclusivity

FABRi designs for real bodies, real movement, and real access needs. Function is central. Difference is treated as a design condition, not a problem to be solved later.

Creativity

FABRi allows imagination to sit alongside use. Each piece holds space for expression, symbolism, and narrative, while remaining grounded in function.

How FABRi Works

Series, Not Products

FABRi organises its work through series, not collections or campaigns.

A series is a way of responding to a cultural question using material. It allows FABRi to move across forms while maintaining a coherent practice.

  • Fashion — what we wear, how we appear, how bodies move
  • Spaces — how environments hold people
  • Objects & Print — what we touch, hold, circulate, and keep

A single cultural moment may activate one or multiple series at once.

FABRi Within Turubai

FABRi sits within Turubai as the practice that translates collective ideas into material form.

It allows people to engage with cultural questions through use, movement, and presence.

FABRi does not resolve tension.
It designs within it.
