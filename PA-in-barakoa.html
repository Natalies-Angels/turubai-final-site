BARAKOA: Love, Fear, and the Quiet

The Play Operating System (Play OS)

What This Play Is

Barakoa: Love, Fear, and the Quiet is a live theatrical work staged inside a Barakoa gig.

It is not only a story that unfolds on stage — it is a living system that the audience enters, observes, and moves alongside.

The play explores how a human being learns to survive the world by putting on different masks.

These masks help us cope with pressure, expectation, danger, and intimacy.

Over time, however, the same masks that protect us can also separate us from ourselves.

  • What does love look like when it is real?
  • What happens to fear when it is managed instead of felt?
  • What becomes of quiet in a world that never stops demanding performance?

The play is reflective and intimate, with moments of dry humor and calm directness.

Its stylistic influence comes from works like The Wonderful Story of Henry Sugar — clear narration, steady pacing, and a voice that calmly explains even as the meaning grows deeper and more unsettling.

The Central Story

The story follows Mwanadamu (MD) — a word that simply means human being.

MD moves through the world carrying a wooden backpack known as the Barakoa Bag (B-Bag). Inside the bag are masks.

At first, the masks feel useful. They make life easier. They reduce friction. They help MD function.

Over time:

  • The masks are used more often
  • Multiple masks begin to operate at once
  • The human underneath becomes harder to access

The play does not judge this process.

It shows it.

ABOUT THE BARAKOA WORLD

These rules apply to all performances, adaptations, and future stagings of the play.

Mwanadamu (MD)

  • MD represents the unmasked human state
  • MD is slow, vulnerable, and real
  • MD can only exist when no mask is active

When a mask is worn, MD is not present.

The Masks

Masks are full identity systems.

When active, they take control of the body and voice.

Multiple masks can operate at the same time.

When this happens:

  • The human presence disappears completely
  • The system becomes efficient but unreachable

Love, Fear, and the Quiet

There are two versions of these states:

Real Love, Fear, and Quiet

  • MD is present
  • Genuine interaction is possible

Illusion Love, Fear, and Quiet

  • Masks dominate
  • Everything feels resolved, but nothing truly is

This distinction is the emotional engine of the play.

Structure of the Play

Total runtime: approximately 80 minutes.

There is no intermission.

Act I — Orientation

“How a human learns to function.”

Act II — Escalation

“When coping becomes control.”

Act III — Aftermath

“What remains when the system is quiet.”

This structure is fixed. The system remains.
