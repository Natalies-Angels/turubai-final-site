What is Barakoa?

Barakoa is a (a 23+) immersive popup experience, happening in Eldoret. A temporary world built from temporary boxes.

It is a space to explore emotional masks; the ways we hide our true feelings from ourselves and others.

Barakoa is a ritual of release. A container to express what we often suppress in daily life: vulnerability, tenderness, fear, longing, and love.

It is designed for adults who are ready to engage emotionally, creatively, and physically — without spectacle, without pressure, and without needing to perform clarity.

Through live music, theatre, fashion, installations, and ritual play, Barakoa creates space to soften what we suppress.

Theme for Barakoa 2026:
Love, Fear & the Quiet.

Why Barakoa?

Barakoa is a Swahili word for masks. The ones we wear to survive. To belong. To stay safe. To be seen...or to disappear.

In daily life, these masks are often invisible to us. We wear them instinctively, switching them on and off depending on where we are, who we’re with, and what we fear losing.

Barakoa exists to bring awareness to these masks, and let them soften through art.

For this edition, the experience is shaped around six emotional masks that many of us move through in relationships, community, and solitude.

Barakoa is a ritual for our times. A space to feel deeply, express honestly, and connect authentically.

Exclusive Experience

On June 19th, Barakoa hosts an invite-only experience for a limited group of attendees who will also have full access to the main gig on June 20th.

This night is structured as a hybrid of:

  • a panel discussion, in partnership with Drunken Lectures
  • beer crafting and tasting
  • live sound and performance
The Panel (Lecture) Theme — Oontzology

The lecture explores Oontzology — the ontology of oontz — looking at groove, repetition, rhythm, and how people gather around sound.

Your Role on June 19th

You will be present on ground with the band and the vocalists. This sound crew will be active in the panel, to give a live demo of the lecture topic in a way that fits naturally in the conversation.

During the panel and lecture, we would like you to be one of the panelists by backing the vocalists and band, playing live within the flow of the lecture, and conversation.

Mic checks and sound checks will also serve as ambience as people arrive.

This is a live illustration of what we are talking about — rhythm, groove, voice, and presence.

Your contribution helps translate theory into feeling.

June 20
Evening Performance — Pre-Dunda into Dunda Session

Format: Live Performance

Function: Hype & Transition

Total Segment Duration: ~3 Hours

Your Set: ~approx. 2 Hours

On June 20th, we want you as a core presence in a two-hour dunda segment people get into to close the night.


This segment will succeed a long-form performance by the band and vocalists who'll deliver a 'coke-studio' kind of collaborative performance. You will come in about 30 minutes before this session ends, to give a natural transition into the DJ-led party segment.


This segment sits between ritual and celebration.

Your Role on June 20th:
  • DJing with Barakoa material as the core
  • Blending original beats with known tracks
  • Demo'ing how the beat behaves on the dancefloor
  • Holding flow across long sessions

On this day, the dunda session will be a silent disco. This means that this segment will be played through bluetooth headphones that will be provided to the audience.

The headphones allow switching between three channels which we have named:

  • the love channel,
  • the fear channel,
  • and the quiet channel, in alignment with Barakoa's message of the day. Each channel will carry a different vibe.

One channel will have your live set, while the others play your pre-made Barakoa playlists

Sound & Curation

Ahead of June, the Barakoa beat is built digitally, and mostly virtually.

This process brings together:

  • orchestra and vocal recordings
  • textures and loops
Your Role in this Phase:

  • Receive and organize sound contributions from collaborators
  • Mix, layer, and shape these into a coherent Barakoa beat
  • Ensure the beat works across speakers, headphones, quiet and speaking spaces.

What you help build here becomes:

  • playlists for the day
  • stems for performance including the live theatre, a key feature of the day.
  • and the sound language the whole sound team works from

Creatives’ Meet & Greet

Your presence in this won't be a performance. It's more:

This is fully dependent on sponsors, as we intend for it to be a fully funded experience, with all creatives compensated for their energy.

PS: During the life cycle of the gig, you will have moments of playing solo as well as moments backing the orchestra and the vocalists
