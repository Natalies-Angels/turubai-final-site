Barakoa

Barakoa is a masquerade.

Not just in costume, but in behaviour, protection, performance, and silence.

It explores the masks people wear around love, fear, and the quiet things we don’t say — and what happens when those masks are held, softened, or briefly removed.

Barakoa brings together sound, movement, fashion, and space to ask one central question:

How do we mask ourselves in order to survive love, fear, and vulnerability?

  • Six behavioural archetypes, expressed through masks
  • A shared dress code that turns the audience into part of the world
  • An original Barakoa sound language
  • A live, embodied environment

Turubai Studios enters Barakoa to give this question a voice.

How Turubai Studios Enters Barakoa

Through sound and silence.

Turubai understands sound not just as background music or technical support, but as a core part of how Barakoa works. In Barakoa, sound helps shape how people feel, move, gather, and pause.

Sound plays a role in how themes like love, fear, and quiet are experienced. It can amplify emotion, create comfort, create tension, or help people disconnect and reconnect

Turubai Studios works with a mixed sound team — DJs, sound engineers, vocalists, and orchestral musicians — to make sure sound is intentional across the entire experience, not just during performances.

Sound in Barakoa is used across live performances, theatre and spoken moments, party sessions, and in quiet and reflective spaces.

Just as important, Turubai Studios also enters Barakoa through silence.

The sound of silence in Barakoa

Silence is part of the sound design.

This is why the Barakoa experience ends with a silent disco.

The silent disco allows each person to choose what they are listening to(everyone will have a set of bluetooth headphones), instead of everyone hearing the same thing at the same time. It turns listening into a personal decision.

The headphones allow switching between three listening channels:

  • the love channel
  • the fear channel
  • the quiet channel

Each channel represents a different emotional state.

The quiet channel is not empty. It is designed to create space — for reflection, rest, and attention — especially after a long day of stimulation.

The silent disco helps Barakoa hold different experiences at once:

  • Being together, while listening individually
  • Ending the event without noise taking over
  • Letting people leave with their own thoughts

This is how Turubai Studios brings both sound and silence into Barakoa.

The Barakoa Beat: Nilofunktronica

The central aesthetic theme of Barakoa is Nilofunk. It is the aesthetic language guiding Barakoa’s music, fashion and styling.

Nilofunk a fusion of:

  • Nilo — Nilotic cultural references featuring traditional motifs across Nile communities
  • Funk — the bold, expressive silhouettes and textures of 1970s–80s African American funk aesthetics
  • Contemporary Finish — modern reinterpretation of Nilo and Funk aesthetics, blending traditional elements with contemporary design

Using this logic, Turubai Studios developed an original Barakoa sound system called Nilofunktronica.

Nilofunktronica is a contemporary fusion of:

  • Nilo — traditional Nilotic rhythm, repetition, and communal logic
  • Funk — groove, bass, guitar, and body intelligence
  • Tronica — electronic texture, loops, and spatial control

This beat is not one song. It is a system that allows original compositions, existing songs, live performance, remixes, edits, and moments of silence to coexist.

Source: Anyiny Tumdo by Kalya Traditional Dancers

Source: The Maasai Footsteps Show

How the Barakoa Beat Comes to Life

The Barakoa beat is something we actively build before the event — and then perform, remix, and stretch on the day itself.

Because sound carries the whole experience, the beat needs to exist in two forms:

  • Digitally — as playlists, stems, and mixes used throughout the day
  • Live — as something musicians, vocalists, and DJs can perform and reinterpret in real time

What we build before the gig becomes the sound language everyone works from during Barakoa.

Building the beat (before the gig)

The Barakoa beat is built mostly virtually.

Each part of the sound team responds to the Nilofunktronica theme in their own way, then shares their work digitally.

These pieces are sent online, reviewed, combined, and refined until they feel like one shared world — not separate contributions.

How each sound role contributes...upon agreement

1. Orchestra — feeling and structure

The orchestra brings emotion, tension, and atmosphere.

  • Create musical parts inspired by Nilofunktronica
  • Send recorded pieces that help shape the Barakoa beat
  • Perform live during the event — alone, with vocalists, and/or over DJ rhythms (as agreed)

2. Vocalists — voice and story

Vocalists bring language, emotion, and presence.

  • Contribute original vocal ideas or interpretations
  • Record pieces that become part of the beat-building process
  • Perform live — solo, in collaboration, or backed by orchestra and DJ (as agreed)

3. Sound Engineer / Producer — unity

The sound engineer is the glue.

  • Collect recordings from orchestra, vocalists, and collaborators
  • Mix, layer, and shape them into a coherent Barakoa beat
  • Ensure the beat works across speakers, headphones, live rooms, and silence

4. DJ — movement and translation

The DJ brings flow and familiarity.

  • Blend the Barakoa beat into known songs, hits, and bangers
  • Test how the beat behaves on the dancefloor
  • Perform live by weaving original Barakoa material with existing music

How the beat is used during Barakoa

Once built, the Barakoa beat becomes the base for everything.

It is stretched across:

  • Theatre moments
  • Panels and talk spaces
  • Live performances
  • DJ-led party sessions
  • The silent disco experience

Each context stretches the sound differently. What holds it together is intention.

The Barakoa Listening Book

All of this sound work is documented in The Barakoa Listening Book.

The Listening Book is a record of:

  • Research and references
  • Original beats and mixes
  • The story behind each mix
  • How to listen
  • Clear credit to all contributors

The Listening Book holds the process, not just the outcome. It becomes an archive of how Barakoa 2026 sounded, what it explored, and what it left unsaid.
