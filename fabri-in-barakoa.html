Barakoa

Barakoa is a masquerade.

Not in costume alone; but in behaviour, protection, performance, and silence.

It explores the masks people wear around love, fear, and the quiet things we don’t say — and what happens when those masks are held, softened, or briefly removed.

Barakoa brings together sound, movement, fashion, and space to ask one central question:

How do we mask ourselves in order to survive love, fear, and vulnerability?

  • Six behavioural archetypes, expressed through masks
  • A shared dress code that turns the audience into part of the world
  • Original Barakoa theme and beat (Nilofunk)
  • A live, embodied environment

Fabri enters Barakoa to give this question a body.

How Fabri Enters Barakoa

Fabri is responsible for crafting the physical language of the maskerade.

Fabri is responsible for translating Barakoa’s ideas into material form. It explores how masking looks like when it's worn, layered, adjusted, removed, or lived in.

Fabri chose to enter Barakoa primarily through fashion and styling because clothing is where masking is most intimate:

  • it sits on the body
  • it regulates exposure and protection
  • it moves with emotion, sound, and time

Fashion becomes the first site of meaning.

The Barakoa Aesthetic Theme: NiloFunk

NiloFunk is the aesthetic language guiding Barakoa’s music, fashion and styling.

It’s a fusion of:

  • Nilo — Nilotic cultural references featuring traditional motifs across Nile communities
  • Funk — the bold, expressive silhouettes and textures of 1970s–80s African American funk aesthetics
  • Contemporary Finish — modern reinterpretation of Nilo and Funk aesthetics, blending traditional elements with contemporary design

What Fabri borrows from Nilotic dress logic :
the 'Nilo' in NiloFunk

Nilotic communities across the Nile corridor dress with deep awareness of climate, easy movement, ceremony, and protection.

Key Nilo principles Fabri draws from:

Blanket logic:

the use of cloaks, shukas, shawls,(draped, not layered) etc as outerwear for protection, warmth, and modesty

Layering:

the strategic use of multiple garments to create depth, warmth, and visual interest. Nilotes layer garments to adapt to climate and ceremony.

Flow and volume:

clothing that responds to wind, walking, dancing, sitting

Adornment as signal:

beads, metal, leather used to mark status, ritual, or moment — and removed when needed

The Masks Behind The Story

Barakoa is anchored by six behavioural masks:

  • The Keeper
  • The Justifier
  • The Silencer
  • The Savior
  • The Mirror
  • The Ghost

These masks are not characters — they are patterns people recognise in themselves.

Fabri responds to these masks in three ways:

  • Costumes for actors and performers — designed to embody each mask’s behaviour
  • Audience masks(for the masquerade) — simplified, wearable interpretations available with tickets
  • Barakoa Fashion Line — a six-look collection that reflects the masks' essence

Attendees will read the mask descriptions and choose the one that feels closest — or select a general mask for those who don’t wish to name themselves.

This way, the experience becomes a shared, personal journey of self-discovery.

How Everything Ties Together

Fabri applies Nilofunk across the entire experience ... not only on stage.

1. Team Dress Code

The creative team behind Barakoa becomes part of the visual language.

Each creative behind Barakoa should wear an outfit that reflects the Nilofunk aesthetic, reinforcing the immersive experience.

Fabri will guide:

  • Color palettes
  • Fabric choices
  • Styling tips
  • Weather-adaptive options

This is done with respect to each team member's budget and access through offering a range of options from thrifting advice, custom looks, personal wardrobes, etc.

2. Audience Dress Code

The dress code is shared publicly as part of Barakoa’s storytelling.

It invites attendees to participate creatively, interpret Nilofunk in their own way, and arrive already inside the world.

3. Costumes for Performers

Fabri designs costumes for the actors and performers built from:

  • mask behaviour
  • Nilofunk silhouette
  • movement needs

4. The Maskerade Masks

Fabri designs simplified, wearable versions of the six behavioural masks for audience members to wear during the maskerade.

These masks are designed to be comfortable, breathable, and easy to wear for extended periods.

5. The Barakoa Fashion Line

On the day of the gig, Fabri will release six outfits, each inspired by one mask.

These outfits will be available for purchase online and at the event, allowing attendees to embody their chosen mask through fashion.

These pieces:

  • Reflect Nilofunk aesthetics
  • interpret love, fear, and quiet through form

THE NILOFUNK LOOKBOOK

All of this research, styling logic, and visual language will be documented into a Nilofunk × Barakoa Lookbook.

This book serves multiple purposes:

  • A reference guide for the creative team to maintain visual coherence
  • An inspiration source for attendees to interpret the dress code
  • A record of the collaboration between Fabri and her partners.
  • A cultural artifact in its own right

Creative collaborators involved will be credited as official partners of this lookbook.
