How You Come In

Dear Drunken Lectures,

We’re reaching out to explore a partnership with you around Barakoa — a conscious, culture-led gig built within Turubai, and hosted in collaboration with NUKe, a Turubai Lab project focused on sound, culture, ritual and underground creative practice.

We’re hosting two live editions in Eldoret in 2026: a teaser in March and a main edition in June... and we’d love to build with you as part of how consciousness shows up inside those gigs.

Below is a brief on Barakoa, the experience, and where you come in. We hope it resonates.

What is Barakoa?

Barakoa is a (23+) immersive popup experience. A temporary world built from temporary boxes.

It is a space to explore emotional masks; the ways we hide our true feelings from ourselves and others.

Barakoa is a ritual of release. A container to express what we often suppress in daily life: vulnerability, tenderness, fear, longing, and love.

It is designed for adults who are ready to engage emotionally, creatively, and physically — without spectacle, without pressure, and without needing to perform clarity.

Through live music, theatre, fashion, installations, and ritual play, Barakoa creates space to soften what we suppress.

Theme for Barakoa 2026:
Love, Fear & the Quiet.

Why Barakoa?

Barakoa is a Swahili word for masks. The ones we wear to survive. To belong. To stay safe. To be seen...or to disappear.

In daily life, these masks are often invisible to us. We wear them instinctively, switching them on and off depending on where we are, who we’re with, and what we fear losing.

Barakoa exists to bring awareness to these masks, and let them soften through art.

For this edition, the experience is shaped around six emotional masks that many of us move through in relationships, community, and solitude.

Barakoa is a ritual for our times. A space to feel deeply, express honestly, and connect authentically.

The Barakoa Experience

March: private, invite-only teaser ritual.

June: full ceremony. All boxes active.

Barakoa is built by a tribe of creatives, cultural curators, and underground influencers who move across music, fashion, visual art, and digital culture.

Why We're Reaching Out,

We’re reaching out because we’d like Drunken Lectures to feature within both Barakoa gigs, as a structured, intentional lecture moment that fits the rhythm and politics of the experience.

Drunken Lectures sits at a rare intersection we care deeply about:

  • • clarity without moralising,
  • • intelligence without stiffness,
  • • and learning that doesn’t interrupt enjoyment... it deepens it.

That alignment is why we see Drunken Lectures as a natural collaborator within Barakoa’s conscious design

Drunken Lectures in the Teaser Gig

Topic:
Oontology: The Ontology of Oontz

For the March edition, Drunken Lectures would feature as a live lecture moment focused on The Ontology of Oontz.

At this stage, Barakoa is actively building an original sound identity for the gig -- what we’re calling the Barakoa beat.

This process draws from:

  • • Nilotic musical traditions,
  • • AfroFunk structures and groove memory,
  • • Repetition, trance, and neurological response to rhythm
  • • Dance-floor psychology

The lecture becomes a way to open up this process publicly, as an accessible, playful dissection of why certain sounds move bodies, how rhythm becomes culture, and how underground music carries meaning beyond entertainment.

This lecture aligns with:

  • • Live sound exploration happening at the gig
  • • Early audience exposure to the Barakoa sonic language
  • • Drunken Lectures’ ability to make complex ideas land lightly

Drunken Lectures in the Main Gig

Topic:
The Mole, Alcohol, and I

This session would be an exclusive, intimate Drunken Lecture experience, hosted ahead of the main (June) gig.

This session grows out of very real nights ... mixing drinks without understanding how alcohol actually works, forgetting to hydrate, misjudging strength, and paying for it the next day.

The Mole, Alcohol, and I uses simple science and honest experience to unpack how alcohol concentration, mixing, dilution, and pacing affect the body, so people can make better choices while still enjoying themselves.

The session would be structured as:

  • • Live beer crafting and tasting
  • • Practical discussion around mixing, pacing, and awareness

This lecture aligns with:

  • • Learning how to drink and mix consciously, without killing the vibe
  • • Understanding alcohol in a way that prevents hard comedowns and regret
  • • Barakoa’s commitment to informed enjoyment and care for the body

Both Barakoa editions will feature Drunken Lectures in a panel-style format, with each session tailored to its moment and purpose
