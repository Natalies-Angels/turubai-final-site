You in Barakoa

You participate in Barakoa through a partnership as a Dramaturge — with your primary vehicle for this creative catalysis being Script Writing.


In this role, you are the architect of the play's linguistic world. You aren't just writing lines; you are building the narrative and intellectual logic that anchors every performance. You shape the what, the why, and the how by translating dramaturgical research into the literal text of the Barakoa Play.

Dramaturgy via Script, explained

As a Script-centered Dramaturge, you bridge the gap between abstract concept and live performance by:

  • Building Character Logic: Developing the internal voice and dialogue for MD and the various "Masks" in the B-Bag.
  • Structural Rigor: Organizing scenes and movements to ensure the narrative architecture remains coherent across the 4-act system.
  • Tonal Consistency: Maintaining the instructional, calm, yet unsettling voice inspired by stylistic references like Henry Sugar.
  • Contextual Writing: Crafting Security’s monologues so they function as both house rules and essential narrative world-building.
  • Collaborative Refinement: Working with artistic leads to ensure every word serves the survivor-centered inquiry of Perpetrators Anonymous.

Your work is where the philosophy of Barakoa becomes audible and actionable for the performers and the audience.

Your Contribution

You will support the storytelling ecosystem by:

  • / Developing the primary script for the live Barakoa experience.
  • / Translating dramaturgical research into performable dialogue and stage directions.
  • / Designing the verbal interactions between the "System" (Security) and the audience.
  • / Reframing accountability and harm through nuanced, context-grounded storytelling.
  • / Refining the script during the rehearsal process to meet the needs of the live environment.

Recognition

You will be credited as a Dramaturge & Script Writer across all official Barakoa publications, digital platforms, and production credits — acknowledging your dual role in shaping both the meaning and the voice of the work.
